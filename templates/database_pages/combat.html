Combat System

Battle Overview

Clash of Code battles are fast, tactical duels between two AI cores. Your bot's stats, algorithm, and weapon work together to decide who acts first, who lands the cleanest hits, and who runs out of power.

Turn Flow

Each round, both bots get a chance to act. Higher Clock Rate (CLK) usually means acting first, which can be the difference between a clean knockout and a narrow loss. If both bots are equally fast, the order can shift from round to round.

Energy and Endurance

Every action consumes energy. A bot with a strong Power Core (PWR) can stay active longer and keep pressure on the opponent, while low-energy builds risk stalling out before the fight is decided.

Damage, Dodges, and Critical Hits

Damage scales with Processing Output (PROC) and is reduced by Defense (DEF). High Entropy (ENT) leans into risk: critical hits can swing a round, but they never replace good fundamentals like solid defense and speed.

Faster bots are harder to pin down. If your CLK outpaces your opponent, you'll notice more clean hits and fewer returns.

Signature Protocols

Some bots carry special protocols that can trigger under pressure. These are rare, dramatic moments that can change the pace of a fight. Treat them as bonuses, not guaranteed wins.

Arenas

The battlefield matters. Some arenas favor melee, some favor ranged, and others test pure stability. Check the Arenas page to see which environments best suit your build.

Combat Logs

After each battle, the combat log records the key events in order. It's the best way to understand how your build performs and where it needs tuning.
