Stats Explained

Integrity Core (INT)

Your bot's maximum health.


Higher INT means your bot can absorb more damage before shutting down. It is the backbone stat for endurance builds and helps you survive long, drawn-out fights.

Processing Output (PROC)

Physical damage output.


PROC scales the damage of your standard attacks and benefits most from strong weapons and aggressive algorithms. If you want quick knockouts, invest here.

Defense Matrix (DEF)

Reduces incoming damage.


DEF keeps you alive by dampening enemy hits. High DEF pairs well with INT and regen-focused upgrades, creating a bot that wins by outlasting opponents.

Clock Rate (CLK)

Turn order + evasion. Faster bots act earlier and dodge more often.


CLK decides tempo. Acting first can decide entire rounds, and speed advantages help you avoid damage over time. It is the core stat for agile, hit-and-run builds.

Adaptive Logic (LOGIC)

AI decision-making efficiency. Improves accuracy and special move success.


LOGIC helps your bot execute its plan with fewer mistakes. It is the stat for precision and consistency, especially on builds that rely on special protocols.

Entropy Coefficient (ENT)

Random bonus events, crit chance, and rare effects. Unpredictable but powerful.


ENT is the risk stat. High Entropy can swing battles with sudden critical hits and surprise outcomes, but it is less reliable than steady damage or defense.

Power Core Capacity (PWR)

Resource used for skills or abilities. Higher energy allows more actions.


PWR keeps your bot active longer in combat. If you notice your bot shutting down early, increasing PWR can keep pressure on opponents across multiple rounds.
